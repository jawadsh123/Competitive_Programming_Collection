This year,Hogwarts has been nominated to host the coveted Triwizard Tournament. Harry has been selected to represent Hogwarts at the tournament.Being the hosts,the stakes are high & Harry doesn't want to disappoint anyone.

Harry in the second task in his quest to win the Triwizard Cup needs to rescue his friends which have been captured by the Merpeople.

Viktor Krum rescued Hermione whereas Cedric Diggory rescued Cho Chang.Unfortunately,Fleur Delacour was attacked by the grindylows & could not complete the task.Harry now needs to rescue both Ron & Gabrielle Delacour from the wrath of the sea.

The Merpeople would only allow Harry to rescue one of them.However,they would allow Harry to rescue both of them only if he solves a puzzle.

The puzzle is as follows:

  • Given a rectangular paper of length L and breadth B, we needs to cut a square piece out of it such that the square has side of maximum possible length.
  • Atleast one side of this square needs to be touching an edge of the initial paper.
  • After cutting, there will be a certain amount of paper left. We needs to carry out the same steps on this piece that remains after cutting.
  • This process needs to be carried on until there is no paper left.
  • At the end, we need to state the unique lengths of all square that were cut, and the number of squares that were cut of that length (frequency).
  • These lengths and the corresponding freauencies needs to be printed in descending order of length.

Time is running low for Ron & Gabrielle, Harry needs to quickly solve the problem but he's not really good with puzzles. So he's asking you to solve the problem for him.

Help Harry solve the puzzle & save both Ron & Gabrielle.

 

Input

The first line of the input contains an integer T denoting the number of test cases.
The next T lines contains 2 space separated integers L and B denoting the initial length and breadth of the paper.

Output

For each test case print k lines with two integers, side-length of square (Si) and number of squares with side-length Si, i.e. (Ci), in descending order of their side-lengths, where k is the no of squares with unique side-length

Constraints

Should contain all the constraints on the input data that you may have. Format it like:

  • 1 ≤ T ≤ 104
  • 1 ≤ L ≤ 1018
  • 1 ≤ B ≤ 1018

 

Example

Example 1

Input:
1
6 5

Output:
5 1
1 6

Example 2

Input:
1
4 2

Output:
2 2

 

Explanation

Example case 1.

  • Initially the paper is of dimension 6 x 5
  • The largest square that can be made in this state is of dimension 5 x 5
  • After cutting out the 5 x 5 square, we're left with paper of dimension 5 x 1
  • Now the maximum size square we can make is 1 x 1
  • After removing 1 x 1 we are left with 4 x 1 paper. Again the largest square that can be made is of 1 x 1
  • We carry this process until we are left with no paper.
  • So we made 1 square of length 5 and 6 squares of length 1

Example case 2.

  • Initially the paper is of dimension 4 x 2
  • The largest square that can be made is if dimension 2 x 2
  • After removing the 2 x 2 square we are left with 2 x 2 paper
  • Since this is itself a square, it becomes the current largest possible square so we cut it out.
  • After above step we are left with no paper
  • So we made 2 squares of length 2
