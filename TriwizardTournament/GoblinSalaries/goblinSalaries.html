Whoa! There seems to be a conundrum at the Gringotts Wizarding Bank. The Goblins have gone on a riot and the entire system is in mayhem.

Reports suggest that the Goblins have not been getting proper salaries lately. The Enchanted Calculator seems to be broken. Since the calculation of Goblin salary is a really tough task, no mortal has taken up the responsibility.

Septina Vector, the famous Arithmancer has been called in for help. You are interning under her and will assist her in this task.

You have the following info:

  • Each Goblin is assigned a unique positive integer
  • A Goblin's salary = a function f(x), where x = number assigned to that goblin.
  • f(x) = Sum of all the prime factors of x
  • Only those Goblins with numbers in range of L to R (both inclusive) are participating in the riot. And all of the Goblins in that range is present.

The Ministry of Magic wants to stop the riot and bring everything back to normal (as normal as it can get)

Your goal is to find the sum of the salaries of all the Goblins present in the riot so that proper amount can be withdrawn from the bank for distribution.

 

Input

The first line of the input contains an integer T denoting the number of test cases. The description of T test cases follows.

Each test case contains two space seperated integers L and R denoting the range of numbers assigned to the Goblins present in the riot.

 

Output

For each test case, output a single line containing the total amount that needs to be withdrawn from the bank in order to pay all the Goblins present in the riot.

 

Constraints

  • 1 ≤ L ≤ R ≤ 1012
  • R - L ≤ 106
  • 1 ≤ T ≤ 5

 

Subtasks

  • Subtask 1 (20 points): 1 ≤ L ≤ R ≤ 103
  • Subtask 2 (30 points): 1 ≤ L ≤ R ≤ 106
  • Subtask 3 (50 points): Original Contraints

Example

Input:
2
1 10
2 13

Output:
45
76

 

Explanation

Example case 1.
For L=1 and R=10 we have
f(1)=0 (as 1 is not a prime)
f(2)=2 (as 2=2*1 and 2 is prime)
f(3)=3 (as 3=3*1 and 3 is prime)
f(4)=2+2=4 (as 4=2*2 and 2 is prime)
f(5)=5 (as 5=5*1 and 5 is prime)
f(6)=2+3=5 (as 6=3*2 and 2 and 3 are prime)
f(7)=7 (as 7=7*1 and 7 is prime)
f(8)=2+2+2=6 (as 8=2*2*2 and 2 is prime)
f(9)=3+3=6 (as 9=3*3 and 3 is prime)
f(10)=2+5=7 (as 10=5*2 and 5 and 2 are prime)
sum of range from f(1) + f(2) + f(3).....+ f(10)= 45
